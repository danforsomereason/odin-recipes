Recipe for Stagnancy


Get out of here and check out our recipe for success!

Description

There are times in life where it feels great to be cozy. Chilly fall nights by the fireplace, Christmas Eve with loved ones, or with your favorite comfort food recipe. The recipe described here seems the same, but it's different. Because this feels so good, we sometimes romanticize the feeling and follow behaviors to get that contented feeling. But over time, we confuse the feeling of comfort with the feeling of happiness.

Stagnacy is defined as a state of inactivity, boredom, or depression, or a lack of growth and development. That makes it seem obvious, though sometimes it's not. Happiness is a difficult concept to grasp. It cam seem elusive. Most people confuse pleasurable feelings with happiness. Happiness is more like a state than an emotion. It's earned through accomplishment, connection with others, and reducing the number of things you want vs the things you have. Stagnancy feels good in the short term, but can drift us away from happiness.

Ingredients

  • Complacency
  • Endless Tutorials
  • Comfort
  • Rumination
  • Procrastination
  • Optional: Compare yourself to others.

Recipe

  1. Start by gathering all the ingredients from Recipe for Success and set aside.
  2. Procrastinate (here's a defintion if you need some help: "A voluntary and irrational tendency to delay required tasks despite knowing that the delay will result in negative consequences such as negative emotions (e.g., guilt, anxiety, depression, stress) and/or negative results [e.g., disappointing self/others, performance evaluations, or other losses."])
  3. Copy AI-generated code and follow tutorials mindlessly.
  4. Tell yourself that you'll start sometime soon.
  5. Make lots of preparations to work on things.
  6. Spend lots of time looking up how to stop not doing things.
  7. If comparing yourself to others, whisk together the desire to have what they have without doing the work it took them to get there. Complain.
